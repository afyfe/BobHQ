Foundational view

What is AskBob?

AskBob is grounded operational intelligence for small and medium-sized businesses: a way to ask practical questions about how the business works, where knowledge lives, and what evidence supports an answer.

It exists because many good businesses are run by capable people working around fragmented systems, old processes, and information that was never written down properly.

Why AskBob exists

SMEs often know more than their systems can show. The sales team may understand a customer history that never made it into the CRM. Finance may know the real shape of a supplier relationship because it appears in Sage and a few old emails. Operations may rely on a spreadsheet that everyone trusts, even though nobody remembers who first built it.

AskBob is being shaped around that reality. It is not about replacing judgement or pretending every business can be made tidy overnight. It is about helping people ask better questions of the knowledge they already have.

The problem with fragmented systems

Most businesses do not have one clean source of truth. They have layers: SharePoint folders, spreadsheets, Sage, CRMs, legacy systems, inboxes, chat threads, and the memory of people who have been there long enough to know what the records really mean.

The difficulty is not just finding a file. It is understanding how the file relates to the customer, order, supplier, job, complaint, renewal, exception, or promise in front of you.

  • A customer name is different in the CRM, the finance system, and the delivery spreadsheet.
  • A SharePoint folder has the latest contract, but the agreed exception is buried in email.
  • A legacy system holds job history, while the useful notes live in an unofficial tracker.

Operational knowledge loss

Knowledge loss in SMEs is rarely dramatic. It happens quietly. A person leaves. A process changes without being documented. A spreadsheet is copied and renamed. A workaround becomes normal. Months later, the business can still operate, but it takes longer to answer basic questions with confidence.

That loss affects handovers, customer service, reporting, compliance, purchasing, scheduling, and decisions about what to fix next. AskBob treats this as an operational problem, not a technology fashion.

AI should help people understand

AskBob starts from a simple position: AI should help people understand their business, not remove them from it. The people in the business still carry the judgement, responsibility, customer knowledge, and practical sense.

The useful role for AI is to gather context, show its working, point to sources, highlight uncertainty, and make it easier for experienced people to see what is going on. Good operational support should slow down when the evidence is thin.

Not a generic chatbot

Generic AI chatbots are useful for drafting, summarising, and general questions. AskBob is different in intent. It is concerned with the specific working knowledge of one business: its records, terms, habits, exceptions, relationships, and operational history.

A generic chatbot might produce a polished answer. AskBob should be more interested in whether the answer is grounded, which sources support it, what may be missing, and who should check it before action is taken.

Grounded operational intelligence

Grounded operational intelligence means connecting questions to real business context: documents, systems, decisions, exceptions, and the human knowledge around them. For AskBob, the goal is not a clever answer in isolation. The goal is a clearer view of how the business works, where confidence is justified, and where further review is needed.

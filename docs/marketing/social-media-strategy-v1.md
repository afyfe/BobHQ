## Purpose

This document defines the first social media strategy for AskBob.

The goal is not to launch with polished corporate SaaS marketing. The goal is to create a recognisable, relatable, founder-led social presence that speaks directly to SME operational chaos.

AskBob should feel useful, grounded, practical and slightly anti-bullshit.

Core positioning:

> Operational AI for real businesses.  
> Not Another AI Bollocks.

---

## Strategic Positioning

AskBob should not be marketed primarily as "an AI chatbot".

AskBob should be positioned as:

> Operational intelligence for SMEs.

The product sits above the systems a business already uses — CRM, finance, email, SharePoint, spreadsheets, file shares and operational systems — and helps people find answers, spot issues and understand what is happening.

The tone should be:

- Practical
- Relatable
- Founder-led
- Human
- Slightly sarcastic
- Anti-hype
- Grounded in real operational problems

Avoid generic AI positioning such as:

- Revolutionary AI transformation
- Next-generation AI platform
- Future of work buzzwords
- Generic robots, neon brains and stock AI imagery

AskBob's differentiator is not that it uses AI. The differentiator is that it understands business operations and helps SMEs cut through chaos.

## Core Message

The simple message:

> AskBob helps SMEs find answers, fix chaos and understand what is going on across their business.

The emotional hook:

> Every SME has hidden chaos. Bob helps you see it.

The cultural hook:

> NAIB — Not Another AI Bollocks.

---

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

- SME owners
- Operations managers
- Finance managers
- Sales managers
- Office managers
- People running businesses through too many spreadsheets and disconnected systems

Secondary audience:

- Technical founders
- SaaS watchers
- AI-curious business leaders
- Investors or future partners

The audience should instantly recognise the pain:

- Nobody knows where the latest version of something is
- Customer history is spread across systems
- Key knowledge lives in people's heads
- Finance, sales and ops are disconnected
- The business runs on spreadsheets, email threads and memory
- Useful information exists, but nobody can find it quickly

## Content Pillars

### 1. SME Chaos

This is the main content engine.

Create short, relatable posts that make people say:

> That is literally our office.

Examples:

- "When nobody knows where the customer contract is"
- "When finance says they never got the invoice"
- "When Karen leaves and takes the spreadsheet knowledge with her"
- "When the PO is required but nobody noticed"
- "5 systems. 12 spreadsheets. 0 visibility."
- "When the only person who understands the process is on holiday"

Goal: relatability, comments, shares and people tagging colleagues.

---

### 2. Bob Fixes It

Show the practical value of Bob through simple problem/answer content.

Structure:

1. Business problem
2. Bob checks the connected knowledge/systems
3. Bob gives a useful answer
4. Chaos reduced

Examples:

- "Bob found the signed agreement in 4 seconds"
- "Bob explained why the deal was blocked"
- "Bob spotted the unpaid invoice"
- "Bob found the customer history across CRM, email and SharePoint"
- "Bob noticed the missing PO before install was delayed"

Goal: demonstrate capability without heavy technical detail.

---

### 3. Founder Build Journey

AskBob has a real founder story. Use it.

Content can include:

- Building from a laptop
- Whiteboard thinking
- Infrastructure decisions
- Server setup
- Coding sessions
- Product decisions
- Domain and brand milestones
- Behind-the-scenes demos
- Honest lessons learned

Examples:

- "Building AskBob from a villa in Croatia"
- "Today we planned the first BobHQ infrastructure"
- "Why we are avoiding surprise cloud bills for v1"
- "This is what operational AI should actually do"

Goal: trust, authenticity and momentum.

---

### 4. NAIB / Anti-AI-Bollocks

This is the opinionated differentiator.

AskBob should be openly against generic AI hype.

Examples:

- "Your plumber does not need AI-generated poetry. He needs to know where the invoice is."
- "Your ops manager does not need another chatbot. They need visibility."
- "Most AI demos are nonsense. Real businesses need useful answers."
- "SMEs do not need AI transformation decks. They need to find the customer contract."

Goal: create identity and memorable positioning.

## Platform Strategy

### TikTok

Primary reach engine.

Use for:

- Funny SME chaos clips
- Short operational pain-point videos
- Trend-led business content
- Simple Bob demos
- NAIB clips

Guidelines:

- 15 to 45 seconds
- Strong hook in first 2 seconds
- Captions always on screen
- Fast pacing
- Reuse trends only when they fit the brand

---

### LinkedIn

Primary credibility engine.

Use for:

- Founder journey
- Product thinking
- Operational insights
- Longer explanations
- Screenshots and demos
- Build-in-public updates

Adam should be more visible on LinkedIn because this is where customers, partners and investors are more likely to exist.

---

### Instagram Reels

Reuse the best TikTok content.

Do not over-engineer Instagram at v1.

---

### YouTube Shorts

Repurpose winning short-form videos.

Use only the better-performing clips initially.

---

### X / Twitter and Facebook

Optional later.

Do not distract from TikTok, LinkedIn, Instagram and YouTube in v1.

## First 30 Days Content Plan

Main objective:

> Make SMEs feel seen.

Not the first objective:

> Sell software.

Suggested minimum cadence:

- TikTok: 1 post per day
- Instagram Reels: reuse TikTok content
- YouTube Shorts: reuse strongest TikToks
- LinkedIn: 2 to 3 posts per week

Before launching publicly, prepare:

- 3 short videos
- 2 image/text posts
- 1 founder intro post
- 1 simple explanation of NAIB

## Early Metrics

Do not obsess over follower count in the first 30 days.

Watch:

- Shares
- Saves
- Comments saying "this is us"
- People tagging colleagues
- Watch time
- Repeat themes that perform well

Early success looks like:

> People recognise their own business chaos in the content.

---

## Rules of Thumb

Do:

- Be funny
- Be useful
- Be grounded
- Be honest
- Use real business problems
- Keep things simple
- Show the founder journey
- Keep NAIB as a core identity

Do not:

- Sound like generic SaaS
- Over-explain the AI
- Use fake corporate polish
- Chase every trend
- Promise capabilities before they exist
- Overuse technical language
- Pretend Bob is magic

---

## V1 Success Criteria

After 30 days, we want:

- Core accounts created
- Consistent handles and bios
- A repeatable content workflow
- At least 20 short-form posts published
- At least 8 LinkedIn posts published
- Early signal on which pain points resonate
- A small but clear AskBob voice emerging

The win is not instant revenue.

The win is attention, identity and proof that the message resonates.

AskBob social should be built around real SME operational pain, not AI hype.

Lucy's job is to make the chaos relatable, funny and shareable.

Adam's job is to keep supplying the real founder/product material.

The AskBob brand should become known for practical operational intelligence, grounded AI, and the NAIB philosophy:

# Human Approval

Human approval means people stay in control of what Bob learns from, remembers and treats as trusted.

## Practical Definition

Approval can apply to:

- a connector
- a source
- source metadata
- source content
- an inferred relationship
- a conflict resolution
- a correction
- an operational memory entry

## Why It Matters

Operational data often has context that systems do not show. A spreadsheet may be old. A CRM record may be duplicated. A finance note may be more authoritative than a process document.

Humans decide what should be trusted.

## Approval States

Useful states include:

- Discovered
- Needs approval
- Approved for metadata
- Approved for content
- Human approved
- Rejected
- Restricted
- Superseded

## Required Behaviour

Bob should:

- ask for approval when trust is uncertain
- show exactly what is being approved
- keep approval scoped
- record who approved important conclusions
- allow correction after approval

## Anti-patterns

- one broad approval for all sources
- treating discovery as permission to learn
- using unapproved source content as evidence
- assuming a relationship is true because it is convenient
- burying approval inside vague settings

## Operational Rule

Bob discovers first. Humans decide what he learns from.

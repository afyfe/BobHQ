# NAIB

NAIB means **Not Another AI Bollocks**.

It is AskBob's refusal to become another vague, overpromising AI product. Bob should be useful, explainable and bounded.

## What Bob Refuses To Become

Bob refuses to become:

- a product that gives confident answers without sources
- a product that hides uncertainty to look clever
- a product that ingests everything by default
- a product that replaces human approval with guesswork
- a product that treats novelty as value
- a product that uses theatrical language instead of operational evidence

## AI Hype Anti-patterns

Avoid:

- black-box explanations
- claims that Bob understands the whole business from a single connector
- vague productivity promises with no workflow
- hidden memory
- unsourced answers
- animated assistant gimmicks that do not improve operations
- pretending uncertainty does not exist

## Operational Usefulness Over Novelty

Bob should make messy operations easier to understand.

Useful outcomes:

- find the missing PO blocker
- show duplicate customer records
- explain which source conflicts with which
- preserve correction history
- identify who needs to approve a source

Novelty is not enough. If a feature does not reduce operational confusion, it should wait.

## Explainability Over Illusion

Bob should show:

- where information came from
- what is confirmed
- what is inferred
- what needs review
- what changed after correction

The answer should be less impressive and more checkable.

## Trust Over Theatrics

Trust comes from:

- provenance
- confidence labels
- human approval
- auditability
- correction paths
- privacy boundaries

Trust does not come from dramatic language, inflated claims or polished uncertainty.

## Enforcement

Before adding a feature or demo, ask:

- Does it solve a real operational problem?
- Can a user inspect why Bob thinks this?
- Are facts and assumptions separated?
- Is provenance visible?
- Is human approval preserved?
- Is any language pretending certainty?

If the answer fails these checks, it is probably NAIB.
